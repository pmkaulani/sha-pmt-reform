SHA PMT v2.1 — Means-Testing Reform

Comprehensive Briefing: Gaps, Evidence & Solutions

Peter M. Kaulani  |  KCA University  |  June 2026



The Social Health Authority’s Proxy Means Testing (PMT) algorithm is failing Kenyans at scale. The May 2026 joint Africa Uncensored / Lighthouse Reports investigation "Error by Design" proved with field data that the current Lasso-based PMT overestimates income for the poorest households. Of more than 20 million SHA-registered citizens, only 5 million are regularly paying premiums. Ninety-two percent of private providers report financial distress from delayed reimbursements. The system is bleeding trust and revenue simultaneously.

This briefing documents what has been built, what was found upon deep audit, what evidence was missed, and the exact fixes needed for a bulletproof reform proposal that survives parliamentary scrutiny.

1.  WHAT WAS BUILT — THE AGI MODEL V2.1



SHA PMT v2.1 replaces intrusive housing proxies with a four-source triangulation engine: Safaricom M-Pesa retained balance, KRA PIN registration, NTSA TIMS vehicle registry, and self-declared household data. Deductions are applied to produce an Adjusted Gross Income (AGI). The legally mandated 2.75% rate is applied to AGI, not gross income.

2.  WHAT WAS OVERLOOKED — TEN CRITICAL GAPS



2.1  Code vs. Documentation Mismatch — The Soft-Landing Formula

CRITICAL BUG (Embarrassment Risk) gen_assumptions.cjs documents a soft-landing formula: max(300, (agi - 131,000) x 0.01). This does NOT exist in AlgorithmSimulation.js, which uses raw 2.75%. At AGI = KSh 200,000 the documented formula gives KSh 690/month, the code gives KSh 458/month. Any engineer, analyst, or journalist reading both files will spot this immediately.



Fix: Either implement the soft-landing formula, or remove it from the Assumptions Register and document why raw 2.75% was chosen (it is mathematically smooth: 2.75% of 131,000 = exactly KSh 300.20). The mismatch is the problem, not the choice of formula.

2.2  SHA Act 2023 Legal Tension — Gross vs. Adjusted Income

HIGHEST LEGAL RISK Section 27 of the Social Health Insurance Act 2023 mandates 2.75% of "gross household income." The AGI model applies 2.75% to an adjusted figure after significant deductions. A government lawyer can attack the entire proposal on this single point: you are proposing to collect less than the statutory minimum. This is not addressed in any of the 20 existing documents.



Fix: Option A: Argue "income" in Section 27 must be interpreted through Article 43 (right to health) and Article 27(4) (non-discrimination) to mean ability-to-pay income. Requires a legal opinion. Option B: Frame deductions as "assessed liability reductions" applied after the 2.75% is computed. Both need a one-page constitutional law memo before any submission.

2.3  Seasonal Worker Zero-Balance Exploit

MEDIUM-HIGH RISK (Fraud Surface) When lowSeasonRetainedBalance is declared as 0, Math.min(effectiveBalance, 0) = 0 produces annual income = 0, guaranteeing indigent classification. No floor exists, no verification flag fires. It is a free pass to subsidy for anyone who learns about it.



Fix: Apply KSh 500 minimum floor and trigger requiresChpVerification = true for zero declarations. See Section 4, Fix 1 for exact code.

2.4  SACCO Account Referenced But Ignored in AGI

MEDIUM RISK (Wealth Signal Gap) hasSaccoAccount appears in Digital Ghost detection but contributes nothing to AGI. A citizen with KSh 2M in SACCO share capital and zero M-Pesa balance is classified indigent. Kenya has 5.7 million SACCO members. SHA opponents will cite this as a major uncovered wealth gap.



Fix: When hasSaccoAccount = true, require declared SACCO share capital as an input field. Add 50% of declared capital to AGI (other 50% treated as illiquid savings). Cross-validate via KUSSCO API when available.

2.5  Revenue Impact Field Is Methodologically Unsound

MEDIUM RISK (Credibility) calculateProposedModel() returns revenueImpact = (monthlyContribution x 12) x 1.15, a flat 15% compliance uplift applied per-household with no cited source. This is not how revenue modelling works. A KIPPRA reviewer will reject the entire submission if this appears in a presentation.



Fix: Delete revenueImpact from per-household output entirely. Revenue projections belong exclusively in generateRevenueStressTest() with its five cited-source scenarios.

2.6  IDInsight Pre-Deployment Report Was Never Obtained

CRITICAL STRATEGIC GAP Reuters and Guardian (May 2026) revealed that SHA's own consultancy IDInsight warned the system was "inequitable for low-income households" before launch, and government deployed it anyway. This was obtained via public access request. The reform project never filed for it. It is the single most powerful evidence in existence: SHA knowingly deployed a system its own evaluators said would harm the poor.



Fix: File a Public Information Request under Access to Information Act 2016, Section 8, to SHA CEO Mercy Mwangangi for the full IDInsight pre-deployment evaluation report and any SHA response. File this week. Once obtained, cite it as Exhibit A in every document.

2.7  The Court Deadline Is Not Leveraged

STRATEGIC GAP (Urgency Framing) The March 2026 High Court ruling declared the SHA rollout unconstitutional and issued a 90-day compliance deadline, falling this month. The court mandated quarterly progress reports and required SHA to show no patient is denied emergency care due to algorithm failures. Not one document in this project opens with this framing. That is why government has not responded.



Fix: Every submission must open with: "This proposal responds to the High Court's March 2026 constitutional compliance directive requiring SHA to demonstrate that no patient is denied emergency treatment due to contribution algorithm failures. The AGI model is that remedy."

2.8  County-Level Rejection of SHA PMT Not Addressed

IMPLEMENTATION GAP A June 2026 NCBI peer-reviewed study found multiple Kenyan counties have abandoned the SHA PMT and are using their own targeting methods, citing exclusion risks, manipulation, and failure to capture locally-defined poverty. Counties may not adopt the AGI model without a specific county-level strategy.



Fix: Add a County Government Buy-In Protocol to the Implementation Roadmap. Propose piloting in Turkana (ASAL), Kiambu (urban satellite), Mombasa (coastal urban). The ASAL discounts are a direct response to county objections; position them as county-designed features.

2.9  Mixed Formal-Informal Households Not Modelled

COVERAGE GAP Formal employees already have 2.75% deducted at source. The AGI model applies only to informal households. Mixed-income households (salaried spouse + informal trader) are unaddressed. This gap creates inconsistency during implementation and will be cited by SHA defenders.



Fix: Define a hybrid rule: if any household member has verifiable PAYE deductions via KRA iTax API, their formal contribution is deemed satisfied. Only informal income of other members is assessed via AGI. Total = formal PAYE + AGI-based informal, subject to household maximum of 2.75% of combined income.

2.10  No Confidence Intervals on Income Estimates

ACADEMIC AND LEGAL RISK The algorithm produces exact point estimates (e.g. "AGI = KSh 247,500") from uncertain proxy data. Lighthouse Reports specifically criticised PMT models for treating estimates as certainties. For appeals, SHA must defend a precise number it cannot prove. KIPPRA reviewers will flag this.



Fix: Assign a confidence tier (HIGH/MEDIUM/LOW) based on verified data sources and output AGI as a range: LOW: +/-35%, MEDIUM: +/-20%, HIGH: +/-10%. For appeals, the range is the reviewable bound, not the point estimate. See Section 4, Fix 3 for code.

6.  REMAINING BLIND SPOTS — WHAT CANNOT BE SOLVED YET



Blind Spot

Why It Cannot Be Solved

Current Mitigation

Mixed Formal-Informal Households

SHA Act does not define the contribution split for hybrid households; KRA API cannot expose per-member salary without legislative amendment.

Hybrid rule (Section 2.9) is a working approximation pending legislation.

Polygamous Household Income Splitting

IPRS shows multiple household linkages but cannot distinguish genuine financial integration from separate households for ASAL pastoralists.

Fraud Flag 18 exists but will produce false positives for legitimate polygamous families. Needs a pastoralist exception.

Diaspora Cash Remittances

Physical cash or bank transfers from diaspora are invisible to the M-Pesa API. The diaspora deduction field can be gamed.

Requires CBK/Treasury integration with international transfer records. Not achievable in current architecture.

Informal Market Traders (Cash-Only)

A trader with KSh 200K/month turnover holding all earnings as cash has zero digital footprint and self-declares KSh 0.

Digital Ghost + CHP protocol exists. Requires adequate CHP staffing, a county resourcing problem outside the algorithm.

KNBS Data Staleness

AGI parameters use KNBS 2024 poverty line estimates. CoL tiers will become stale without annual recalibration.

Recommend tying all CoL parameters to KNBS CPI in the Implementation Roadmap with an annual update cycle.
