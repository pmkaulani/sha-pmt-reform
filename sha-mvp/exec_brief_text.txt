SHA PMT v2.1 — Executive Briefing

A Reform Proposal to Fix Kenya's Health Insurance Means-Testing Algorithm

Peter M. Kaulani  ·  KCA University  ·  June 2026



1.  THE PROBLEM



The Social Health Authority (SHA), launched October 2024, sets health insurance premiums for Kenya's 83% informal-sector workforce using a Proxy Means Testing (PMT) algorithm. The investigation Error by Design (Africa Uncensored · Lighthouse Reports · The Guardian, May 2026) and a pre-deployment IDinsight report — obtained through access-to-information requests — both confirm the algorithm was known to be flawed before launch. IDinsight warned it was "inequitable, particularly for low-income households" and depended on an out-of-date 2020 survey. SHA deployed it anyway.



Core Algorithm Flaws (28 identified)

• M-Pesa gross velocity treated as personal income (Chama trap)

• Stone walls / tile roofs add KSh 120–150K to wealth score

• Electricity access penalised as "luxury" (+KSh 80K)

• ASAL land and livestock valued at full national market rate

• Static per-head dependent deduction (not income-proportional)

• No Catastrophic Health Expenditure (CHE) deduction

• No appeal mechanism embedded in model logic

Documented Real-World Impact

• Farmers' predicted incomes double their actual earnings (Lighthouse Reports audit)

• Dialysis & cancer patients losing treatment access after overclassification

• Only 3.1M of 19M+ registrants completed means-testing by Feb 2025

• SHIF annual deficit: KSh 38.3 billion (Auditor-General, March 2026)

• KSh 11B lost to hospital-side fraud (Oct 2024–Apr 2025): adverse selection accelerates this

• Informal sector contributed only KSh 5.72B of KSh 57.7B collected in FY2025



Note on KSh 11 Billion Fraud: This loss came from rogue hospitals fraudulently billing SHA on the claims side. It is a separate but related problem. The PMT reform addresses the enrollment side: correct assessments → fair premiums → more voluntary enrolment → lower adverse selection → less incentive for hospital fraud.



2.  THE SOLUTION: ADJUSTABLE GROSS INCOME (AGI) MODEL

Preserves the statutory 2.75% rate — fixes the 28 systemic flaws in the base it is applied to



Replace intrusive housing proxy variables with digitally triangulated income signals cross-referenced via KRA, NTSA TIMS, and Safaricom M-Pesa retained balance. The SHA Act 2023 §27 rate of 2.75% is preserved. No legislative amendment is required.

5.  KEY TECHNICAL INNOVATIONS



Triangulation Trinity: KRA × NTSA TIMS × Safaricom. Three independent government data sources audit every self-declaration. Fraud resistance is built in, not bolted on.

8 Vulnerability Exemptions: PWD (30%), Bodaboda (50%), Seasonal workers, Chama treasurers, Diaspora remittances, Refugees/IDPs, SACCO bypass, Consent-withheld. Each mapped to a constitutional or legal basis.

18-Flag Automated Fraud Engine: Detects phantom dependents, income splitting, false commercial claims, undeclared NTSA vehicles, multi-SIM concealment, and KRA income mismatches — all without housing inspections.

Smooth Indigent Transition: 2.75% × KSh 131,000 ÷ 12 = KSh 300.21. The law's flat rate creates a mathematically smooth transition at the indigent threshold. No cliff. No jump.

SHAP Deduction Receipts: Every household receives a plain-language breakdown of every factor that changed their contribution. Constitutional compliance with Art. 35 (Access to Information).

Full DPA 2019 Compliance: §32 consent-withheld pathway, 2-year raw data purge / 7-year outcome retention (§39), audit record per assessment, no automated penalty without human review.



6.  IMPLEMENTATION PREREQUISITES (Non-Negotiable)



Step 1 — API MoU Framework (0–6 months):

SHA must establish formal data-sharing Memoranda of Understanding with KRA, NTSA, and Safaricom under DPA 2019 §19. Without these three APIs, the fraud engine cannot function and the model falls back to pure self-declaration — replicating the current system's evasion vulnerability.

Step 2 — Pilot County — Recommend Nakuru (6–12 months):

6-month live pilot in one Tier 2 county before national rollout. Nakuru offers urban-rural mix, non-ASAL context, moderate fraud risk, and existing SHA infrastructure. Pilot generates real-world data to validate model parameters against ground truth.

Step 3 — SHAP Transparency Obligation (at rollout):

Every household assessment must generate a SHAP deduction receipt — a non-technical explanation of every factor affecting their contribution. Minimum requirement for Art. 35 compliance and the Access to Information Act 2016.



7.  CONSTITUTIONAL & LEGAL GROUNDING



Art. 27(4) — Non-discrimination

Current model produces higher error rates for women, ASAL residents, and rural poor. AGI model includes equalized-odds fairness testing across all demographic groups.

Art. 43 — Right to Health

Overcharging that drives unaffordable premiums and disenrollment directly violates the right to the highest attainable standard of health.

Art. 54 — PWD Protections

30% AGI deduction for NCPWD-registered persons. No provision exists in the current model.

Art. 56 — Marginalized Communities

40% ASAL dependency allowance cap; 85% arid land discount. Protects pastoralist communities systematically excluded by the current model.

DPA 2019

Consent-withheld pathway, tiered data retention, audit records. No automated adverse decision without human-in-the-loop review.

SHA Act 2023 §27 — 2.75% Rate

Fully preserved. The AGI model changes the income base, not the rate. No legislative amendment is required for implementation.



8.  KNOWN LIMITATIONS — HONEST ACKNOWLEDGEMENTS



⚠ API Dependency Risk: Full fraud resistance requires live KRA, NTSA, and Safaricom APIs. Until MoUs are signed, fallback mode (self-declaration + mandatory manual review within 14 days) must be the operational standard.

⚠ Rent Declaration: Actual monthly rent is deducted up to a capped ceiling, but rent is currently self-declared and unverifiable at scale. Mitigation: cross-reference with KPLC meter addresses and county rates databases.

⚠ Cash Economy (Digital Ghosts): Estimated 5–8% of informal workers have near-zero digital footprint. These citizens receive provisional indigent classification pending CHP physical verification within 90 days. SACCO account holders bypass this hold.

⚠ Chama Fiduciary Exemption: The 80% fiduciary exemption prevents Chama treasurer overcharging but is open to false claims. Fraud Flag 14 (Unverified Fiduciary Claim) detects this if Safaricom group registration API is live.
